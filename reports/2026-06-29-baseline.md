> This baseline extends the library's start date from 2020 to **2015** and casts a wide net across journals, registries, regulators, conferences, and reputable general/business press. It fills the previously empty 2015–2019 window and the thin indications (corpus callosotomy, meningioma, lower-grade glioma). Every finding carries an explicit publication date and is scored on two axes — Clinical Impact (★1–5) and LITT Business Impact (0–10 with tailwind/headwind direction). Balanced, evidence-based, non-promotional; established vs. promising vs. speculative are distinguished, and unverified figures are flagged rather than laundered into confident claims.

## 1. Executive Summary

This run added **78 new findings**, bringing the corpus to **136** spanning 2012–2026. Of the new items, 44 read as tailwinds for LITT, 16 as headwinds, the remainder direction-agnostic. The dominant historical theme (2015–2020) is the **shift from whole-brain radiotherapy to SRS-alone** (N0574, N107C, Mahajan, JLGK0901), which is a net **tailwind** for LITT: it spares patients broad irradiation while enlarging the population of focal in-field recurrences and radiation-necrosis lesions that LITT salvages. In parallel, the **foundational LITT evidence base** was backfilled (NeuroBlate first-in-human 2013, the TDT extent-of-ablation framework 2014, the BBB-disruption mechanism 2016, the ≥80% ablation threshold for post-SRS salvage 2016, the LAASR prospective study 2018) and the **epilepsy ablation** literature deepened across SLAH (Willie 2014, Drane 2015, Gross 2018, SLATE 2020), hypothalamic hamartoma (Curry 2018), TSC tubers, and — filling a prior gap — **corpus callosotomy** (Caruso 2018, Roland 2019, Tao SLACC 2020). The clearest **headwinds** are the maturation of **MR-guided focused ultrasound** (Exablate thermal ablation FDA-approved for tremor 2016; BBB-opening drug delivery 2016–2019; a 2025 Lancet Oncology GBM survival signal) and **CNS-active systemic therapy** (osimertinib, tucatinib) that can defer upfront local therapy. The single biggest structural **tailwind** is reimbursement: dedicated Category I CPT codes 61736/61737 (AMA panel action Oct 2020, effective 2022).

## 14. Five Emerging Trends

- **SRS-alone replaces WBRT** for limited and post-operative brain metastases — a durable feeder of radiation-necrosis and focal-recurrence salvage cases for LITT.
- **LITT as an immunotherapy enabler** — BBB disruption (2016 mechanism → 2026 randomized pembrolizumab signal) reframes ablation from cytoreduction to drug-delivery potentiation.
- **Diagnose-and-ablate** — pairing stereotactic biopsy with same-session ablation to resolve recurrence-vs-necrosis, increasingly supported by amino-acid PET selection.
- **Robotics + AI workflow** — ROSA/Renishaw stereotaxy and AI segmentation/lesion-localization lower procedural friction and widen the candidate pool (epilepsy SLAH, tubers, callosotomy).
- **MR-guided focused ultrasound** maturing as the incisionless thermal-ablation / drug-delivery competitor to watch.

## 21–24. Questions Physicians Are Asking · Conversation Starters · Common Misconceptions · Areas of Controversy

- *Recurrence or radiation necrosis?* — amino-acid PET plus biopsy-and-ablate increasingly resolves the question LITT is uniquely positioned to act on.
- *Does extent of ablation matter?* — TDT-line coverage and the ≥80% ablation threshold correlate with control; under-ablation predicts failure.
- *LITT vs craniotomy vs SRS?* — comparable oncologic outcomes for deep/eloquent and post-SRS lesions; open surgery relieves mass effect faster.
- *Misconception:* dedicated LITT CPT codes existed since ~2016 — in fact 61736/61737 were a 2020 AMA panel action, effective 2022.
- *Controversy:* whether focused ultrasound (ablation and BBB-opening) will complement or displace LITT in tumor indications.

## 25. What We Still Don't Know

- No in-window randomized trial of LITT vs craniotomy/SRS with OS endpoints; most LITT evidence is registry/cohort or single-arm.
- Durability of LITT+immunotherapy survival signals beyond small randomized lead-ins.
- Whether adjuvant RT helps after GTR of atypical meningioma (ROAM pending).
- Head-to-head LITT vs MR-guided focused ultrasound for tumor ablation.
