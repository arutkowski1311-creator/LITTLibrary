## Executive Summary

The single most important development for the LITT business is the **Feb 2026 randomized
Phase 1/2b of LITT + adjuvant pembrolizumab in recurrent high-grade astrocytoma**
(median OS 11.8 vs 5.2 mo; 18-mo survival 42% vs 0%) — the first randomized signal that
LITT-mediated blood-brain-barrier disruption can potentiate immunotherapy. It reframes LITT
from cytoreduction to an *immunotherapy enabler*.

Counterweighing this are competitive and modality headwinds: **Visualase V2** (FDA clearance,
Jun 2025) refreshed the #1 rival on a thermometry-accuracy story; **ClearPoint Prism's 1.5T
clearance** (Sep 2025) roughly doubled a third entrant's addressable market; and on the
clinical side, **broadening SRS** (incl. lower-necrosis preoperative/fractionated regimens)
and **TTFields** (METIS phase 3, GBM combinations) are narrowing two LITT strongholds
(radiation necrosis; the non-invasive adjunct narrative).

In epilepsy, the evidence is maturing favorably on LITT's core selling point — **cognitive
preservation** (SLAH vs hippocampus-sparing ATL) — even as a 3,873-patient meta-analysis
honestly quantifies LITT's modestly lower seizure-freedom vs open resection (52.5% vs 67.1%).
The clearest defensible niche emerging across tumor work: biopsy-confirmed, SRS-refractory
recurrence or radiation necrosis in deep/eloquent locations, selected with PET + AI
volumetrics, and increasingly paired with systemic/immunotherapy.

## Five Emerging Trends
1. **LITT as an immunotherapy enabler** (BBB disruption + checkpoint blockade).
2. **Diagnose-and-ablate** same-session robotic biopsy + LITT.
3. **Cognitive-preservation positioning** in dominant-hemisphere MTLE.
4. **AI volumetrics + PET** driving selection and post-ablation surveillance.
5. **Competitor ecosystem bundling** (robot + navigation + LITT from one vendor).

## Five Potential Threats to LITT
1. Visualase V2 thermometry differentiation. 2. ClearPoint Prism 1.5T market expansion +
ecosystem. 3. Broadening / lower-necrosis SRS eroding the radiation-necrosis niche.
4. TTFields owning the non-invasive adjunct narrative. 5. No national reimbursement coverage.

## Five Opportunities for LITT
1. Own the LITT+immunotherapy story (Nat Commun data). 2. Cognitive-sparing epilepsy
referrals. 3. Radiation-necrosis salvage where SRS/re-RT fail. 4. Deep/eloquent minimally
invasive access (safety + cost). 5. Pipeline of comparative RCTs (LaSAR BeaM, Tiantan).

## Questions Physicians Are Asking
- Does LITT really potentiate immunotherapy, or is the control arm just too small?
- Is lower seizure-freedom vs open resection worth the cognitive/recovery benefit?
- After broadening SRS, when does a lesion still "need" LITT?
- Is it recurrence or radiation necrosis — and can PET/AI decide without biopsy?

## Common Misconceptions
- "LITT is only cytoreduction" — emerging data position it as an immunotherapy enabler.
- "LITT equals open surgery on seizure freedom" — meta-analysis shows a real, if modest, gap.
- "Radiation necrosis is rare" — it is a growing, under-recognized SRS sequela.

## Areas of Controversy
- LITT vs open resection seizure-freedom trade-off. LITT vs bevacizumab vs surgery for
  radiation necrosis (no head-to-head RCT). Whether broadening SRS net-shrinks LITT's niche.
  Strength of the small-control-arm immunotherapy signal.
